  1. What would be the mean and median gallons flushed if we combined the two data sets into one large data set with 39 observations? Show how the mean can be calculated from all observations in one vector and also with the summary measures in part (c) along with the sample sizes. Explain why the median of the combined set cannot be computed based on (c).

Calculating the combined dataset mean and median with all observations:

combined<-c(current, new)
mean(combined)
## [1] 1.327692
median(combined)
## [1] 1.28

The mean can be calculated with part (c) because we know how many values are in the individual datasets and thus the number of values in the combined data. The current mean is \(1.5765\) and the new mean is \(1.065789\). The current data has 20 values, so the sum of all values would be \(1.5765*20=31.53\). The new data has 19 values, so the sum of all values would be \(1.065789*19=20.25\). Then the sum for the combined data would be \(31.53+20.25=51.78\). Then divide by 39, which is the total number of data points in the combined dataset to get, \(51.78/39=1.327692\)

The median, on the other hand, only depends on the middle two values in a dataset. Since we dont have the individual values of combined dataset just by looking at the two medians in part (c), we do not know what those two middle values are, and we do not know those values from just knowing the individual medians. Therefore, the new median cannot be calculated with just the information from part (c).
